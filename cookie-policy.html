Cookie Policy

How we use cookies.

This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how Solutions by Danielle uses them on this website, and the choices you have. It should be read alongside our Privacy Policy. Last updated 20 May 2026.

About cookies.

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make sites work, to remember your preferences, and to provide information to the site owner. Under UK law — the UK GDPR and the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR) — we only set non-essential cookies with your consent.

01 What cookies are +

A cookie is a small file of letters and numbers stored on your browser or device. Similar technologies such as pixels, tags and local storage work in comparable ways, and references to "cookies" in this policy include those technologies.

Cookies may be "session" cookies, which are deleted when you close your browser, or "persistent" cookies, which remain until they expire or you delete them. They may be set by us ("first-party") or by a third party whose service appears on our site ("third-party").

02 The categories of cookies we use +
  • Strictly necessary cookies. Required for the website to function — for example to remember items in a booking enquiry, maintain security, and record your cookie preferences. These do not require consent and cannot be switched off in our systems.
  • Functional cookies. Remember choices you make, such as your preferred region or language, to give a more personalised experience. Set only with your consent.
  • Analytics / performance cookies. Help us understand how visitors use the site so we can improve it — for example which pages are most visited. Set only with your consent.
  • Marketing cookies. Used to measure the effectiveness of our advertising and, where applicable, to show relevant content. Set only with your consent.

We do not sell your personal data, and we do not use cookies to make automated decisions that produce legal effects.

03 Consent & your legal rights (UK GDPR / PECR) +

When you first visit the site you will be shown a cookie banner. Non-essential cookies are not set until you give consent. You can accept all, reject all non-essential cookies, or choose which categories to allow, and you can change your mind at any time.

Where cookies process personal data, the legal basis is your consent for non-essential cookies, and our legitimate interest in operating a secure, functioning website for strictly necessary cookies.

You have the right to access, correct or erase your personal data, to withdraw consent, and to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), the UK's data protection regulator, at ico.org.uk.

04 Third-party cookies +

Some cookies may be set by third-party services we use, such as analytics providers, embedded maps, or social media features. These providers may process data outside the UK; where they do, appropriate safeguards are used. We do not control third-party cookies, so please review the relevant provider's own cookie and privacy notices for details.

05 How to manage or delete cookies +

You can manage non-essential cookies through our cookie banner or preferences link at any time. You can also control cookies through your browser settings — most browsers let you block or delete cookies and warn you before one is set.

Please note that blocking strictly necessary cookies may affect how parts of the site work. Guidance for common browsers is available within their own help sections.

06 Changes to this policy & contact +

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in technology, law or our practices. Any changes will be posted on this page with a revised "last updated" date.

If you have questions about this policy or how we use cookies, please contact us using the details below.
