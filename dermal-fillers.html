Dermal Fillers

Restore volume. Smooth lines. Define your beauty.

What are Dermal Fillers?

Dermal fillers are non-surgical injectables designed to restore volume, smooth wrinkles, and enhance facial contours.

Made with hyaluronic acid — a substance naturally found in the skin — they hydrate and plump areas such as the cheeks, lips, and under-eyes for a youthful, refreshed look.

Benefits of Dermal Fillers

  • Restore Volume — Fillers can restore lost volume in areas like the cheeks and temples, creating a fuller, more youthful appearance.
  • Smooth Fine Lines — Fillers reduce the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles, particularly around the mouth and nose (nasolabial folds).
  • Enhance Facial Features — They can be used to enhance lips, define jawlines, and contour the face for balanced, natural results.
  • Non-Surgical & Quick Recovery — Dermal fillers are minimally invasive with little to no downtime, allowing you to resume daily activities quickly.

Before & Aftercare

Before Your Treatment

  • Inform us if you're considering starting, or are already on, any medication
  • Disclose any cold sores (Herpes Simplex), recent treatments or allergies
  • Avoid alcohol and blood-thinning medications (aspirin, ibuprofen) for 24 hours before
  • Eat a small meal beforehand and arrive well hydrated

✓ Aftercare — Do

  • Apply a cold compress gently to reduce swelling or bruising
  • Stay hydrated and maintain a healthy diet for optimal skin recovery
  • Use gentle skincare products and avoid harsh exfoliants for a few days
  • Sleep with your head slightly elevated the first night to reduce swelling

✕ Aftercare — Avoid

  • Touching, pressing or massaging the treated area for at least 24 hours (unless instructed by your practitioner)
  • Strenuous exercise, saunas, steam rooms or hot showers for 24–48 hours
  • Alcohol and blood-thinning medications (aspirin, ibuprofen) for 24 hours, as they may increase bruising
  • Direct sun exposure, tanning beds and extreme temperatures for a few days

What to Expect

Mild swelling, redness or bruising is normal and should subside within a few days. Results will gradually improve as the filler settles.
